# Battlecard: Apex Air Systems

Apex Air Systems is our most common competitor in central Texas, especially on price-driven
bids. They win on sticker price and lose on everything that happens after the sale. Compete on
the gap, never on their number.

**How they sell.** Aggressive low bids, freely undercutting on per-unit rates. They show up
hardest in competitive renewals (property management, education) and procurement-led industrial
bids where price is the headline criterion.

**Where their bids are thin.** Apex bids routinely **exclude** things customers assume are
included:

- After-hours and emergency response (often no written commitment at all)
- Filter media and condensate treatment on PM agreements
- Compliance and commissioning documentation (IQ/OQ) on installs
- Shorter equipment warranties — commonly 5-year compressor coverage vs our 10

**How to beat them.** Build an apples-to-apples scope matrix and line-item every exclusion. Move
the conversation from price to lifecycle cost and risk: the cost of one unplanned outage, the
risk of failing a licensure audit without documentation, the value of a 10-year warranty. Arm
your champion (usually the facilities or site engineer) to defend continuity to the
finance/asset side.

**Where they genuinely win.** When the buyer owns the decision purely on price and Apex bids
below our cost. Don't chase below floor. Lose well: file the post-mortem, and flag the account
for a service-rescue campaign next season — Apex historically understaffs emergency response, so
the door reopens when a customer gets burned on a callout.
