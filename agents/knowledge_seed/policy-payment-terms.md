# Policy: payment terms

**The standard is net-30.** Every Meridian quote and order form carries net-30 by default.
Holding the standard protects cash flow and keeps the business predictable.

**When a customer asks for net-45.** Larger property and institutional customers often run
net-45 as their own accounts-payable standard. Don't simply concede 15 days of float. The
preferred counter is **net-30 with a 2% early-pay credit** — it gives the customer a reason to
pay on our timeline while signaling flexibility. If a buyer insists on net-45, it can be granted
on strategic accounts, but note it on the deal and weigh it against the discount and the deal
size.

**Phased payments on capital projects.** For large installs and retrofits, splitting payment
across two fiscal years is a legitimate and common close lever — especially for healthcare and
education buyers working within an annual capital budget. Phased terms affect cash timing, so
they require controller review before they go into a proposal. Used well, phased payments often
move a budget-constrained buyer from a trimmed option back to the full-scope option.

**Milestone billing.** Installation and retrofit projects bill on milestones (deposit,
mid-project, commissioning) defined in the proposal. Recurring agreements bill annually in
advance by default, or quarterly on request.

**Record the terms.** Payment terms, any early-pay credit, and any phasing all belong in the
deal record — they're part of the negotiation and the agent and approver both reason off them.
