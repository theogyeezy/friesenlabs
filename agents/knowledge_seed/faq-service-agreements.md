# Service agreements — FAQ

**What's in a standard agreement?** Quarterly preventive maintenance on all covered units,
filter media and belts included, condensate and coil service on the summer visits, and a
documented deficiency list after every round so the customer's capital planning has real data.
Emergency response is bundled at a contractual commitment (commonly 4 hours during business
hours; faster tiers available).

**How long are terms?** One year, auto-renewing unless either side gives notice. Multi-year
terms are available and are the preferred structure for larger sites because they let us hold
pricing and schedule capital work.

**What does a renewal look like?** We open the renewal conversation 60–90 days before
expiration with a quarterly business review (QBR): trailing-twelve-month uptime, average
emergency response time, and any open items. Continuity is the pitch — an incumbent who knows
the equipment and has the uptime record almost always wins on total value even when a
competitor undercuts on price.

**When is a renewal "at risk"?** When a customer takes the contract to competitive bid, or when
they have gone quiet (missed QBRs, no account-manager contact). Both are recoverable, but only
if we get ahead of the price conversation with the value story and, when justified, a loyalty
discount inside policy.
