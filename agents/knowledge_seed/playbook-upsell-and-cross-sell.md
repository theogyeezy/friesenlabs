# Playbook: upsell and cross-sell

The cheapest revenue is the next dollar from a customer who already trusts you. Service visits
generate upsell signals constantly — the discipline is capturing and acting on them.

**Mine the deficiency reports.** Every PM visit produces a documented deficiency list. Worn
door gaskets at one location usually mean the same wear on the same route; an aging unit found
on inspection is a retrofit conversation. Turn the tech's findings into a written, small-dollar
quote fast — these close on goodwill when they arrive before the customer has to ask.

**Bundle the route, not the unit.** If gaskets are worn at three of a customer's locations,
quote all three in one visit. One mobilization, better price, less disruption — an easy yes.

**Use small wins to widen the account.** A clean $850 repair upsell on a 14-location hospitality
account is not about the $850; it's proof of responsiveness that sets up the agreement
conversation across the rest of the estate.

**Scope wants as options, not conditions.** When a site engineer mentions wanting a
cooling-tower fill replacement next quarter, write it into the renewal as a priced option. It
shows you're listening without holding the renewal hostage to it.

**Move fast while the trust is warm.** A follow-up email with a written quote before the
customer's next ops review beats a perfect proposal a week late. Queue it, get any required
approval, and send.
