# Discount authority

Discounting is the fastest way to win a deal and the fastest way to erode margin, so authority
is bounded and the boundaries are enforced, not advisory.

| Discount off list | Who can approve |
|---|---|
| 0–10% | Account executive, at the table |
| Above 10% | VP of Sales sign-off required, in writing |
| Below cost (any %) | Not permitted — walk instead |

**The 10% floor is a hard line.** Any quote, order form, or proposal email offering more than
10% off is routed to the approval queue and held until a human with authority approves it — it
cannot be auto-sent, regardless of the deal's autonomy level. This is deliberate: the discount
guard is independent of deal value, so a small-dollar deal at a deep discount still stops for
review.

**Loyalty discounts** (defending a renewal against a competitive bid) follow the same line. An
8% loyalty discount is inside an AE's authority; a 12% match to a competitor is not and must
clear sign-off — and if 12% is below cost, the answer is no.

**How to use it in a negotiation.** Open above your floor. Trade discount for term length,
multi-site scope, or a no-charge value-add (a year-one filter program, a service credit)
before you trade it for nothing. Document the justification in the deal so the approver can act
fast — "matched Apex per-unit; procurement signs this week at this number" beats a bare
percentage every time.
