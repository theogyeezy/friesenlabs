# Playbook: quote and close

Turning a scoped opportunity into a signed agreement without leaving margin or momentum on the
table.

**Quote the right scope, not the cheapest one.** Lead with the option that solves the problem
correctly (option A). Offer a trimmed option B (e.g. defer a monitoring package to a later
phase) only as a budget bridge, and make clear what option B gives up. Buyers often talk
themselves back to A when payment phasing solves the budget constraint.

**Sequence disruptive work around the customer.** Nights, weekends, and phased cutovers for
occupied or critical facilities. Saying this in the proposal signals you've done this before.

**Get the paperwork moving in parallel.** Vendor packet (W-9, COI, references) to procurement,
COI naming the customer as additional insured to your broker, and any compliance documentation
the facility's auditor will want. These are the silent deal-killers — start them before you're
asked.

**Handle terms deliberately.** Default to net-30. If a buyer pushes net-45, counter with
net-30 plus a small early-pay credit before conceding the float. For large installs, phased
payments across fiscal years are a legitimate close lever — route them through controller
review.

**Respect the approval queue.** Anything proposing more than a 10% discount, or a high-value
stage move you want logged, goes through Greenlight. Write the reasoning so the approver can act
in seconds: who, what number, why now, what's at stake.

**Always confirm the next date.** Board vote, committee meeting, signature deadline — put the
date in the record and work backward from it.
