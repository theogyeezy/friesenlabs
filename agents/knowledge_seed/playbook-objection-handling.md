# Playbook: objection handling

The same handful of objections come up on nearly every deal. Have an honest answer ready for
each — and remember that the stated objection is usually a proxy for something else.

**"You're more expensive than Apex."** Almost always true on sticker, almost never true on
total value. Pull out the scope matrix: after-hours response, filter media, documentation,
warranty length. Reframe to lifecycle cost and the price of a single outage. Don't drop below
floor to match.

**"We need a deeper discount to sign."** You can move up to 10% at the table; beyond that needs
VP sign-off and won't auto-send. Trade discount for term length, multi-site scope, or a
no-charge value-add before trading it for nothing.

**"Your terms are net-30, ours is net-45."** Counter with net-30 plus a 2% early-pay credit
before conceding the float. On strategic accounts, net-45 can be granted — note it on the deal.

**"It's not in this year's budget."** For capital work, offer phased payments across two fiscal
years (controller review required). This frequently moves a buyer from a trimmed option back to
full scope.

**"We're happy with our current vendor."** Don't attack the incumbent. Ask about their written
response-time commitment and their last after-hours callout. Plant the comparison and stay in
touch for the next failure or renewal.

**"We have to take it to bid."** Fine — get ahead of it with the QBR data and a loyalty number
held open through the vote, and make sure your champion has cover on price.
