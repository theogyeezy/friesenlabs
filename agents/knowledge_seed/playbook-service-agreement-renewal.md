# Playbook: defending a renewal

A renewal goes to competitive bid, or a competitor quotes under your rate. This is the most
common contested situation and it is winnable almost every time if you lead with value and get
ahead of price.

**Step 1 — bring the QBR data.** Trailing-twelve-month uptime, average emergency response
time, school days / production hours / patient days with zero HVAC loss. Six years of
incumbency and a 99%+ uptime record is the story; make the numbers concrete.

**Step 2 — line-item the competitor's gap.** A lower bid almost always excludes something:
filter media, after-hours response, condensate treatment. Build an apples-to-apples scope
matrix and hand it to the economic buyer (often a CFO/controller) so the board has cover on
price.

**Step 3 — use a loyalty discount, inside policy.** An 8% loyalty discount is inside AE
authority and is usually enough to defuse a competitive bid when the value story is strong.
Frame it as recognition of a long relationship, not a panic cut. Anything above 10% needs VP
sign-off — and if matching the competitor means going below cost, don't.

**Step 4 — hold the offer through the decision.** Tell the customer the loyalty number stays
open through their board or committee vote, and put it in writing. The facilities committee
usually prefers continuity; they just need the price cover to defend the renewal internally.

**Common shape:** the facilities lead is your champion and wants to keep you; the finance side
needs a defensible number. Arm your champion with the matrix and the loyalty letter.
