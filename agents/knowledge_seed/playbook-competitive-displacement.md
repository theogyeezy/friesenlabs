# Playbook: competitive displacement

When a prospect is using a competitor as a price lever, or shopping your incumbent position,
the worst move is to chase the number. Compete on the things a low bid can't include.

**Diagnose the buyer.** A buyer who shares competitor numbers freely is using you to drive
price — common with lean operations and procurement-led shops. Expect a "best and final"
request. Decide your floor before you walk in, and hold it.

**Reframe from price to lifecycle cost.** Warranty length, response-time commitment, filter
media, documentation, and the cost of a single unplanned outage. A 10-year compressor warranty
against a 5-year warranty is real money; a missing after-hours response tier is a real risk.

**Trade, don't cave.** If you move on price, get something: a longer term, multi-site scope, a
reference commitment. A no-charge value-add (year-one filter program) often closes the gap
without touching the rate.

**Know when to lose well.** If the competitor is below your cost and the buyer owns the
decision on price alone, walk — on friendly terms. File a post-mortem, flag the account for a
service-rescue campaign next season (competitors that win on price often understaff emergency
response), and leave the door open. A clean loss you can re-enter beats a won deal you lose
money on.
