# Onboarding: the pipeline stages

Every deal moves through a fixed set of stages. Keeping the stage honest is what makes pipeline
reporting trustworthy.

- **new** — an identified opportunity with a contact, not yet qualified. A diagnostic visit
  request, an inbound RFP, a lead from a service call.
- **qualified** — we've confirmed there's a real need, a budget, and a buying process. We know
  who signs and roughly when.
- **proposal** — a scoped proposal or quote is in front of the customer. Numbers are on the
  table.
- **negotiation** — the customer is actively working the deal: countering on scope, price,
  terms, or payment timing. Move here the moment real back-and-forth starts.
- **closed_won** — signed. The agreement or project is booked.
- **closed_lost** — lost (to a competitor, to no-decision, or to budget). Always file a brief
  post-mortem: why we lost, and whether to flag the account for a future service-rescue.

**Stage discipline matters.** A proposal under active counter belongs in negotiation, not
proposal — leadership reads the stage mix to forecast. Don't park deals in an early stage to
look conservative, and don't push to negotiation to look busy. The stage should match what the
customer is actually doing.

Open pipeline is the sum of everything not yet closed. That number is only as good as your stage
hygiene.
