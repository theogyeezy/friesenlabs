# Emergency response — FAQ

**What is our response-time commitment?** Service-agreement customers get a contractual
business-hours response — 4 hours is the standard tier, with 2-hour and same-hour tiers
available for critical facilities (operating rooms, data-adjacent cooling, food service). The
commitment is to *on-site arrival*, not resolution; parts lead time is communicated separately.

**How does the on-call rotation work?** A named on-call technician carries dispatch after
hours, with a backup. After a missed-commitment incident, the standing remedy is to review the
rotation, fix the gap, and put a written escalation path in front of the customer — that
written follow-up is what restores trust, not an apology alone.

**What counts as an emergency?** Loss of heating or cooling that affects occupants or product:
a chiller down during peak load, a walk-in cooler losing temperature, a boiler failure in
winter, dock-door heaters out. Comfort complaints and scheduled work are not emergencies and
route to the normal PM queue.

**Why does this matter in a deal?** Response time is the single most-cited reason customers
stay or switch. Lead with the uptime and response numbers from the QBR; a competitor's lower
price rarely includes after-hours response at all — always line-item that gap in a comparison.
