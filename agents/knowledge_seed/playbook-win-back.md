# Playbook: the win-back

An account has gone cold — missed QBRs, an account manager left, nobody picked up the cadence —
and now a competitor is quoting under your rate with the renewal close. These are recoverable,
but only with candor.

**Own the misses.** If the relationship lapsed because of us, say so plainly. Buyers can tell
the difference between an excuse and an honest account of what went wrong. The letter that wins
says "the misses were ours, here is what changes."

**Bring a concrete remedy, not a discount alone.** The standard win-back package is four parts:
(1) a modest service credit on the next term, (2) a named account manager with a contact card,
(3) a written quarterly QBR cadence with dates on the calendar before signature, and (4) any
specific work the site engineer has been asking for, scoped into the renewal as an option, not
a condition.

**Lean on your operational champion.** The site engineer who trusts your techs by name —
closed tickets, no repeat failures — is your inside advocate even when the relationship is cold
at the top. Get the asset manager the continuity story through them.

**Mind the threshold and the audience.** A service credit just over the auto-execute line still
needs approval; queue it early so it clears before the committee meets. And send the win-back to
the right person — the property manager or asset manager, never a tenant-wide blast.

**Timing is everything.** Committees meet on a date. Get the final letter in writing a day
ahead of that date, in policy and approved.
