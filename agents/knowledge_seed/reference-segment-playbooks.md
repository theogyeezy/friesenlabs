# Reference: segment notes

Meridian sells into seven segments. Each has a different buyer, a different trigger, and a
different proof point.

**Property management / REIT** — buyer is a facilities director or property manager; the asset
manager owns budget. Triggers: chiller and RTU failures, tenant-comfort complaints, renewals.
Proof: uptime and tenant-impact avoidance. Watch for win-back situations on cold accounts.

**Industrial / logistics** — buyer is a director of ops; lean, price-driven, negotiates
everything. Triggers: aging rooftop fleets, winter dock-door heater reliability. Proof:
multi-site PM coverage and priority dispatch. Expect aggressive competitive pressure.

**Healthcare** — buyer is a facilities director with a procurement gatekeeper. Triggers: OR
air-handling, licensure audits, negative-pressure suites. Proof: IQ/OQ documentation and
compliance. Long cycles, capital budgets, phased payments.

**Hospitality / restaurant** — buyer is a property or ops manager across many locations.
Triggers: walk-in refrigeration failures, comfort cooling. Proof: fast response and route-based
upsell. Small tickets compound into estate-wide agreements.

**Education** — buyer is a facilities director; CFO and board approve. Triggers: campus RTU
fleets, summer changeover, renewals to competitive bid. Proof: zero school days lost, loyalty
discounting inside policy.

**Office / commercial** — REIT-like; comfort and efficiency drive decisions.

**Municipal / nonprofit** — procurement-heavy, bid-required, slow but sticky once won.
