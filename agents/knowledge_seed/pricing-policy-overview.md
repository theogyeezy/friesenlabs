# Pricing policy — overview

Meridian prices on value and lifecycle cost, not on matching the lowest bid. Three pricing
families cover everything we sell:

- **Repairs:** time-and-materials or a fixed quote, typically $4,500–$18,000 depending on
  scope and parts. Emergency repairs carry a dispatch premium outside business hours.
- **Service agreements:** annual recurring, $18,000–$72,000/yr depending on unit count, site
  count, and response tier. Multi-site and multi-year agreements earn structured discounts.
- **Installation and retrofit:** project-based, $80,000 to $480,000+, priced from equipment,
  rigging (crane days), controls/BAS integration, and commissioning.

Two rules sit above all of this and are non-negotiable:

1. **The discount floor.** No discount above 10% off list goes out without VP sign-off. See the
   discount-authority policy. This is enforced in the system: a quote or email proposing more
   than 10% off is held for human approval and cannot auto-send.
2. **Never below floor to match a competitor.** If a competitor's number is below our cost, we
   hold scope, value-engineer where we honestly can, and walk if we must. Buying a logo at a
   loss trains the customer to expect it and poisons the renewal.

Price is almost always the *stated* objection and rarely the *real* one — uptime, response
time, documentation, and a vendor who picks up the phone are what actually retain customers.
