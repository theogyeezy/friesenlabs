# Onboarding: CRM hygiene

A clean CRM is not busywork — it's what lets the agent answer questions accurately, lets your
manager forecast, and lets the next person pick up your account if you're out. The rules are
simple and non-negotiable.

**Log every interaction as an activity.** Calls, emails, notes, and meetings all become
activity records on the relevant deal and contact, with what was said and what comes next. "Walked
the customer through the COI request; board meets the 24th" is worth ten "touched base" notes.

**Keep the stage honest.** Move a deal to the stage that reflects reality. A proposal that's
being actively negotiated belongs in negotiation, not proposal. Stage accuracy drives the
pipeline reporting leadership uses — don't sandbag and don't happy-ear.

**Record the numbers and the dates.** Amount, discount, payment terms, and the next decision
date (board vote, committee meeting, signature deadline) go in the record. The agent and your
manager both reason off these.

**Write reasoning the way you'd want to read it.** When you draft an approval, the reasoning
field should let an approver act in seconds: who, what number, why now, what's at stake. Lorem
ipsum gets your request bounced.

**One source of truth.** Don't keep deals in a personal spreadsheet. If it isn't in the CRM, it
doesn't exist for the team — and it can't be answered by the agent or defended in a forecast.
