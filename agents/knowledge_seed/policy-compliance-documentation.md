# Policy: compliance and commissioning documentation

For regulated facilities — healthcare, food service, and any site facing a licensure or health
inspection — the documentation we deliver is part of the product, not an afterthought. It is
also a genuine competitive differentiator: low-bid competitors routinely leave it out.

**What's included.** On installs and retrofits for regulated facilities, the package delivers:

- **IQ/OQ commissioning protocols** — Installation Qualification and Operational Qualification
  documentation proving the equipment was installed and performs to specification.
- **Air-balance reports** — test-and-balance results for air-handling and pressure-controlled
  spaces (operating rooms, sterile storage, negative-pressure suites).
- **Filter certifications** — HEPA and terminal-filter certs for the spaces that require them.

**Why it matters in a deal.** A surgical center or medical plaza facing a state health-facility
audit needs this documentation to pass. Selling it as part of the scope — and pointing out that
the competing bid doesn't include it — reframes the conversation from price to risk. The buyer
isn't choosing between two prices; they're choosing between passing an audit and scrambling for
paperwork later.

**How to use it.** When you learn a facility has an audit on the calendar, surface the
documentation requirement immediately and scope it in. Flag it to the controls team early so the
protocols are ready at commissioning, not reconstructed afterward.
