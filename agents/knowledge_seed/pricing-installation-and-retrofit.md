# Installation and retrofit pricing

Capital projects are priced bottom-up from five cost drivers, not from a per-ton rule of thumb:

1. **Equipment** — the units themselves (chillers, RTUs, boilers, AHUs) at current
   manufacturer pricing, with lead time noted in the proposal (often 12–16 weeks for chillers).
2. **Rigging** — crane days, roof access, and after-hours or weekend work to avoid disrupting
   the occupant. A Saturday crane day is common on occupied buildings.
3. **Controls / BAS integration** — tying new equipment into the building-automation system;
   frequently underscoped by competitors.
4. **Commissioning and balancing** — test-and-balance and, for regulated facilities, IQ/OQ
   commissioning documentation. This is a differentiator; price it in and call it out.
5. **Phasing** — a phased cutover (one unit at a time) so the building never loses service.
   Phasing adds cost but is non-negotiable on occupied critical facilities.

**Typical ranges**

- RTU replacement program: $80,000–$220,000 depending on unit count
- Chiller retrofit (magnetic-bearing, BAS, phased cutover): $250,000–$320,000 for a two-unit
  central plant
- OR / cleanroom air-handling install with HEPA and pressure monitoring: $110,000–$140,000

**Close levers** that don't touch margin: phased payments across two fiscal years, a
value-engineering swap (e.g. curb adapters) for a modest trim, and a longer compressor warranty
than the competition. Warranty length is a real lifecycle-cost argument — a 10-year compressor
warranty against a competitor's 5 is worth real money over the life of the equipment.
