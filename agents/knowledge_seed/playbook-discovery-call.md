# Playbook: the discovery call

The first real conversation with a facilities or operations leader. Goal: map their world and
find the wedge, not pitch.

**Open by mapping the estate.** How many sites, how many units, what types and roughly what
age? Who currently maintains them, and what's working or not? You are listening for: aging
equipment with no PM history, a vendor who doesn't pick up after hours, and a recent failure
that scared someone.

**Find the pain that has a date on it.** A chiller that tripped last week, a licensure audit
this fall, a renewal coming up, a budget cycle that closes in July. Pain with a date is a deal
with a timeline.

**Qualify the buying process early.** Who signs? Is there a competitive-bid requirement? Is
procurement involved (vendor packet, PO)? Institutional and municipal buyers almost always have
a process — knowing it on call one saves weeks later.

**Set up the value story.** Plant the seeds you'll harvest later: uptime, response time,
documentation, lifecycle cost. Ask what their current vendor's response-time commitment is —
most don't have one in writing.

**Next step, always.** End with a scheduled action: a site walkthrough, a QBR, or a scoped
proposal with a date. A discovery call with no committed next step is a deal that quietly dies.
