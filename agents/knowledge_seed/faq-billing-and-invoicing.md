# Billing and invoicing — FAQ

**How are agreements billed?** Preventive-maintenance agreements bill annually in advance by
default, or quarterly on request for customers who prefer to match their own budget cadence.
Repairs and on-demand work bill on completion, net-30. Installation and retrofit projects bill
on a milestone schedule defined in the proposal (typically deposit, mid-project, and
commissioning).

**What are our standard payment terms?** Net-30 is the Meridian standard and is what every
quote carries by default. Some larger property and institutional customers run net-45 as their
own AP standard; see the payment-terms policy for how to handle that without simply giving up
30 days of float.

**Can customers phase payments across fiscal years?** For large installs, yes — splitting a
project across two capital years is a common close lever for budget-constrained buyers
(healthcare and education especially). Phased terms need controller review before they go in a
proposal because they affect cash timing.

**What about purchase orders and vendor onboarding?** Institutional and procurement-led buyers
require a vendor packet first: W-9, certificate of insurance, and references. Submit it early —
procurement onboarding is often the long pole on an otherwise-won deal.
